Book Recommendations

Poor Charlie's Almanack

A collection of speeches and talks by Charlie Munger, Warren Buffet's business partner. A great primer on Munger's mental models and investing philosophy.

Ben Franklin's Autobiography

The autobiography is written in a simple langauge. The book demonstrates Ben's thinking style. The book was written in two parts. The first part was written in 1771 and the second part was written in 1784.

How to Win Friends and Influence People

I dismissed this book initially since it seemed to encourage inauthentic behavior. Over time, I found it helpful to understand people better. It's quite useful to read and re-read every couple of years.

Red-Blooded Risk

It's not the easiest read, but their were several concepts that I found no where else. My favorite insight was in a truel (3 person duel), the person who is the best shot has the lowest chance of survival.

Fortune's Formula

The book is about the Kelly Criterion, a formula for determining the optimal size of a series of bets. This book exposed me to Claude Shannon, who is the father of information theory. The key takeaway is that you can go broke even when your expected value is positive.
